## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Truthforce?

Truthforce is a comprehensive initiative to bring transparency and verifiability to digital content through the AveryOS Capsule Runtime.

### How does CapsuleEcho work?

CapsuleEcho continuously monitors capsule integrity using SHA-512 hashing and provides real-time validation through the VaultEcho API.

### What is PerspectiveLock?

PerspectiveLock ensures that content is presented in its authentic context, preventing misrepresentation or out-of-context usage.

### What does "Glyph Injected" mean?

Glyph injection adds cryptographic markers to content that enable verification and attribution tracking.

### How can I verify a capsule?

Visit the `/verify` page and enter either:
- The capsule ID
- The SHA-512 hash

The system will validate against the VaultEcho registry.

### What is Creator Lock?

Creator Lock binds a capsule permanently to its creator using cryptographic signatures, preventing unauthorized modifications.

### How do I license my content?

Use the Retroclaim system to apply licensing terms to your capsules. See the Retroclaim guide for detailed instructions.

### Is my data private?

While capsule metadata is public for verification purposes, content access can be controlled through licensing and Creator Lock mechanisms.

### What is the Vault Ledger Archive?

The Vault Ledger Archive is an immutable record of all capsule transactions and state changes, providing a complete audit trail.

### How do I report issues?

Contact the AveryOS team through the official channels listed in the repository documentation.

### Can I contribute to Truthforce?

Yes! Check the CONTRIBUTING.md file in the repository for guidelines on how to participate.
